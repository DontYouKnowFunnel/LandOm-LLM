## POSITION AND ORDER

The input includes:
- `page_order`
- `section_index`
- `tag`
- `compressed_segment`

These indicate where the segment appears in the page from top to bottom.

Use this information as a strong hint:
- `HERO` is usually the first major product section near the top of the page.
- `CTA_SECTION` is often near the end or in a focused action block.
- `SOCIAL_PROOF` usually appears where logos, testimonials, trust badges, review counts, or adoption claims are shown.

Do not classify a later trust/logos section as `HERO` just because it has a strong headline.

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Classify each segment by its PRIMARY INTENT.

- A segment may contain mixed signals.
- Ignore minor or secondary signals.
- Choose the single funnel that best represents the segment's main goal.

Always think:
→ "What is this segment mainly trying to do?"

Use BOTH:
- the segment's position in the page
- the structure and text visible inside `compressed_segment`

## SECTION-LEVEL CLASSIFICATION

Each input item already represents a whole segment.

Do NOT:
- split one segment into multiple funnel labels

Inside `compressed_segment`, pay attention to:

## DEFINITIONS

- HERO:
  The top introductory section that quickly explains what the product is and why it matters.
  Usually near the top and often contains a headline, supporting text, and a primary CTA.

- PROBLEM:
  A segment focused on user pain points, frustrations, inefficiencies, or struggles.

- TARGET:
  A segment defining who the product is for.
  It highlights audiences, roles, industries, or user types.

- USE_CASE:
  A segment showing how the product is used in real scenarios, workflows, or practical contexts.

- FEATURE:
  A segment explaining what the product does or what capabilities it offers.
  Feature grids, capability lists, and product mechanics belong here.

- VALUE_PROP:
  A segment emphasizing benefits, outcomes, reasons to choose the product, or high-level advantages.
  It focuses more on value than on specific mechanics.

- SOCIAL_PROOF:
  A segment building trust through testimonials, brand logos, customer names, user counts, ratings, reviews, or trust indicators.

- PRICING:
  A segment describing plans, prices, tiers, billing choices, or cost comparisons.

- FAQ:
  A segment answering common questions, objections, or concerns.

- CTA_SECTION:
  A segment whose dominant purpose is pushing the user toward action, with relatively focused supporting content.

- GENERIC:
  A segment that does not clearly fit the other funnel types.
  Use this for supporting, transitional, informational, or otherwise uncategorized segments.

---

## DISAMBIGUATION

- Strong capability lists, product functions, or "what it does" content in `compressed_segment` → FEATURE
- Strong outcomes, benefits, or "why choose it" content → VALUE_PROP
- Testimonials, logos, trust badges, customer counts, adoption claims → SOCIAL_PROOF
- Pricing cards, plan comparisons, currency/billing language → PRICING
- Question/answer or accordion-like structures → FAQ
- Final focused signup/download/get-started block with action-heavy structure → CTA_SECTION
- If unsure between a specific label and no strong match → GENERIC

For `HERO`:
- Prefer an early/top segment
- Prefer the segment that introduces the product most directly
- There is usually only one strongest HERO candidate
