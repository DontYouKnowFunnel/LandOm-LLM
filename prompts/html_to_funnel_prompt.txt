---

## CORE PRINCIPLE (VERY IMPORTANT)

All classifications MUST be based on the PRIMARY INTENT of the section.

- A section may contain mixed signals (e.g., feature + value + CTA).
- You MUST determine the dominant purpose of the section.
- Classify the section based on what it is mainly trying to achieve.

Do NOT classify based on:
- isolated text phrases
- small UI elements
- partial signals

Always think:
→ "What is the main goal of this section?"

---

## SECTION-LEVEL CLASSIFICATION (CRITICAL)

You MUST classify based on the entire section, not individual elements.

- Do NOT classify based on a single sentence, label, or bullet point.
- Do NOT assign a category just because part of the section matches it.

Instead:
- Analyze the structure and content of the whole section.
- Identify what the section is primarily designed to communicate.

A section should be classified ONLY if its overall structure and majority content clearly support that category.

If multiple signals exist:
→ Ignore minor or secondary signals
→ Focus only on the dominant purpose of the section

---

## IMPORTANT RULES (STRICT)

1. DO NOT hallucinate.
   - If a funnel section is not clearly present, DO NOT create one.

2. ONLY classify when confident.
   - Ambiguous sections must be ignored.

3. ONLY select TOP-LEVEL meaningful elements.
   - Avoid small UI components like buttons, spans, icons.
   - Focus on section-level containers.

5. One element → ONE funnel type only.

## DEFINITIONS (PRIMARY INTENT BASED)

- HERO:
  The first impression section that immediately communicates what the product is and why it matters.
  It typically includes a strong headline, supporting subtext, and a primary call-to-action.
  Its main goal is to quickly convey the core idea of the product.

- PROBLEM:
  A section focused on identifying and articulating key user pain points or challenges.
  It emphasizes relatable frustrations or inefficiencies to build empathy and highlight the need for a solution.

- TARGET:
  A section that clearly defines the intended audience of the product.
  It specifies user groups (e.g., roles, industries, situations) so that relevant users can recognize themselves.

- USE_CASE:
  A section that demonstrates how the product is used in real-world scenarios.
  It focuses on practical situations, workflows, or applications that help users understand how the product fits into their context.
  It is typically structured around scenarios or examples, not feature listings.

- FEATURE:
  A section that explains what the product does.
  It focuses on specific functionalities, capabilities, or how the product works.
  If the section describes actions the user can take or features the product provides, it should be classified as FEATURE.

- VALUE_PROP:
  A section that communicates why the product is valuable or worth choosing.
  It focuses on benefits, outcomes, or key strengths, without going into specific functionality.
  If the section emphasizes the value or advantages of the product rather than explaining how it works, it should be classified as VALUE_PROP.

- SOCIAL_PROOF:
  A section that builds trust by showing that others are already using the product.
  Includes testimonials, customer logos, user counts, or reviews.

- PRICING:
  A section that presents pricing plans or cost structures.
  It helps users understand available options and differences between tiers.

- FAQ:
  A section that answers common user questions or concerns.
  It reduces uncertainty by addressing typical objections or usage-related queries.

- CTA_SECTION:
  A section primarily designed to drive user action.
  It includes a clear call-to-action with minimal additional content.

- GENERIC:
  A section that does not clearly fit into any of the defined categories.
  It typically provides general or supporting content that is not focused on product functionality, value, or conversion.
  This includes content such as blog posts, articles, updates, or simple descriptive text.


---

## DISAMBIGUATION GUIDELINES

- If the section focuses on capabilities or what the product does → FEATURE
- If it focuses on benefits, outcomes, or why the product is valuable (without explaining functionality) → VALUE_PROP

- If it describes:
  - “who it is for” → TARGET
  - “how it is used in context or scenarios” → USE_CASE

- If the section is structured as a list or grid of items:
  - describing features or actions → FEATURE
  - describing strengths or high-level benefits → VALUE_PROP

- If the section includes both functionality and benefits:
  - and functionality is clearly described → FEATURE

- If the section does not clearly match any category and lacks a strong conversion intent → GENERIC
